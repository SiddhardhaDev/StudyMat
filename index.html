Mathematics

  1. Division Algorithm: Given positive integers 'a' and 'b', there exists a unique pair of whole numbers 'q' and 'r' satisfying a = bq + r ; 0 ≤ r which is less than b.
  2. The Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic: Every composite number can be expressed (factorized) as a product of its primes, and this factorization is unique, apart from the order in which the prime factors occurs.
  3. If 'p' is a prime and 'p' divides a2, (where 'a' is a positive integer), then p divides a.
  4. Let x be a rational number whose decimal expansion terminates. Then x can be expressed in the form of P/q, where p and q are coprime and the prime factorization of q is of the form 2n 5m, where n and m are non-negative integers.
  5. Let x = P/q be a rational number, such that the prime factorization of q is of the form 2n 5m, where n and m are non-negative integers. Then x has a decimal expansion which terminates.
  6. Irrational number: Let 'n' be any positive number which is not a perfect square then √n is called an Irrational number.
  7. An irrational number is neither terminating nor recurring decimal.
  8. π is an irrational number.
  9. Let x = P/q be a rational number, such that the prime factorization of q is not of the form 2n 5m, where n and m are non-negative integers. Then x has a decimal expansion which is non-terminating and repeating (recurring).
  10. We define log base a of x = n, if an = x, where a and x are positive numbers and a is ≠ 1, n is an element of Real numbers.
  11. Laws of logarithms: If a, x and y are positive real numbers and a is ≠ 1, then log xy base a = log x base a + log y base a.
  12. log (x/y) base a = log x base a - log y base a.
  13. log (xm) base a = m log x base a.
  14. a(log N base a) = N.
  15. log 1 base a = 0.
  16. log a base a = 1.
  17. (log (am) base b) / (log (an) base b) = (m/n) log a base b.
  18. log x base y = (log x) / (log y) (or) 1 / (log y base x).
  19. The arithmetic mean of an ungrouped data: Mean (x̅) = (Sum of all observations) / (No. of observations) = (Σ xi) / n.
  20. Mode of ungrouped data: If an observation occur more frequently in the data or an observation with the highest frequency is called the mode of the data. It is denoted by Z. Data having unique mode is called uni-modal data and the data having two modes is called bi-modal data.
  21. Median for ungrouped data: The given data has to arrange in order of increasing or decreasing magnitude.
    • i) If the number of observations are 'n', and 'n' is odd then the median will be the ((n+1)/2)th observation of the data.
    • ii) If the number of observations are 'n', and 'n' is even then the median will be the average of (n/2)th and ((n/2)+1)th observations of the data.
  22. The mean for grouped data is calculated by: The direct method: x̅ = (Σ fi xi) / (Σ fi).
  23. The assumed mean method: x̅ = a + (Σ fi di) / (Σ fi).
  24. The step deviation method: x̅ = a + (( Σ fi ui) / (Σ fi)) * h.
  25. The mode for grouped data can be found by using the formula: Mode = l + ((f1 - f0) / (2f1 - f0 - f2)) * h, where symbols have their usual meaning.
  26. The median for grouped data is formed by using the formula: Median = l + (((n/2) - cf) / f) * h, where symbols have their usual meanings. Note: In order to find median, class intervals should be continuous.
  27. While drawing less than ogives, upper boundaries are taken on X-axis and cumulative frequencies are taken on Y-axis.
  28. While drawing greater than ogives, lower boundaries are taken on X-axis and cumulative frequencies are taken on Y-axis.
  29. The median of grouped data can be obtained graphically as the x-coordinate of the point of intersection of the two ogives for this data.
  30. Empirical relation among Mean, Median and Mode is: Mode = 3 Median - 2 Mean.

Physics

    • i) A lens may have two spherical surfaces bulging outwards. Such lens is called Double convex lens (or) bi-convex lens.
    • ii) A double concave lens (or) biconcave lens is bounded by two spherical surfaces curved inward.
  2. The distance between the focal point and pole is called focal length of lens denoted by 'f'.
    • i) Any ray passing through the principal axis is undeviated.
    • ii) Any ray passing through at the optic centre is also undeviated.
    • iii) The rays passing parallel to the principal axis converge at focus or seems to diverge from the focus.
    • iv) The ray passing through focus will take a path parallel to principal axis after refraction.
    • v) When parallel rays fall on a lens making certain angle with principle axis, the rays converge at a point or appear to diverge from a point lying on a focal plane.
    • i) The formula used when a light ray enters a medium with refractive index n1 from a medium with refractive index n2 at curved interface with a radius of curvature R is (n2/v) - (n1/u) = (n2 - n1)/R.
    • ii) Lens formula is (1/f) = (1/v) - (1/u). Where, f is the focal length of lens, u is the object distance and v is the image distance.
    • iii) Lens maker's formula is (1/f) = (n-1) * ((1/R1) - (1/R2)). Where, R1 and R2 are radii of curvature of first and second curved surfaces, n is the refractive index and f is the focal length.
    • iv) The Relation between speed of wave (V), wave length (λ) and frequency (ν) is V = λ * ν.
  5. The convex lens behaves as a converging lens if it is kept in a medium with refractive index less than the refractive index of the lens and it behaves like a diverging lens when kept in a medium with refractive index greater than that of the lens.
  6. The frequency of the light remains same while its wave length changes.
    • i) The image formed by actual intersection of reflected rays is called real image. This can be caught on screen.
    • ii) The image formed by extended rays of reflection is called virtual image. It cannot be caught on screen.
  8. All distances should be measured from pole.
    • i) The distance measured in the direction of incident ray to be taken as positive and those measured in the direction opposite to incident ray to be taken as negative.
    • ii) Height of object (ho) and height of Image (hi) are positive if measured upwards from the principal axis and negative if measured downward.
  9. For curved mirrors the radius of curvature will be twice the focal length, R = 2f.
  10. Concave mirror: A spherical mirror, whose reflecting surface is curved inwards, that is, faces towards the centre of the sphere, is called a concave mirror.

Chemistry

  1. A chemical equation is the symbolic representation of a Chemical Change or Chemical Reaction.
  2. A balanced chemical equation explains about Reactants, Products and their Physical States during the reaction.
  3. A chemical equation should always be balanced according to the Law of Conservation of Mass.
  4. The chemical reaction in which heat is absorbed are called Endothermic Reactions and in which heat is evolved are Exothermic Reactions.
  5. "Q" is heat energy which is shown with (+) plus sign on product side for exothermic reaction and (-) minus sign on product side for endothermic reactions.
  6. A chemical equation containing simply symbols of elements and molecular formulae of the substance without balancing is known as Primary (or) skeleton equation.
  7. The symbols of elements and formulae of the compounds should not be changed but only their coefficient should be altered while balancing the chemical equation.
  8. Acid - base indicators are dye (or) mixtures of dyes which are used to detect the presence of acids & bases.
  9. Acidic nature of a substance is due to the formation of H+ ions in solution. Formation of OH- (aq) ions in solution is responsible for the basic nature of a substance.
  10. The substances whose odour changes in acidic (or) basic medium are called olfactory indicators.
  11. Onion, vanilla essence and clove oils are examples for olfactory indicators.
  12. When an acid reacts with metal gives corresponding salt and Hydrogen
    • Eg: Acid + Metal → Salt + Hydrogen
    • Zn (s) + 2HCl (aq) → ZnCl2 (aq) + H2 (g) ↑
    When a base reacts with metal gives corresponding salt and Hydrogen
    • Eg: Base + Metal → Salt + Hydrogen
    • 2NaOH (aq) + Zn (s) → Na2ZnO2 (aq) + H2 (g) ↑
  13. When a base reacts with a metal carbonate (or) metal hydrogen carbonate gives the corresponding salt, Carbon dioxide and water.
    • Eg : Metal Carbonate + Acid → Salt + Carbon dioxide + Water
    • Na2CO3 + 2HCl → 2NaCl + CO2 + H2O
    Metal Hydrogen Carbonate + Acid → Salt + Carbon dioxide + Water
    • NaHCO3 + HCl → NaCl + CO2 + H2O
  14. When an acid reacts with base form salt and water
    • Acid + Base → Salt + Water
    • HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H2O
  15. Acidic and basic solutions in water conduct electricity because they produce Hydrogen ions (H+) and Hydroxy ions (OH-) respectively.
